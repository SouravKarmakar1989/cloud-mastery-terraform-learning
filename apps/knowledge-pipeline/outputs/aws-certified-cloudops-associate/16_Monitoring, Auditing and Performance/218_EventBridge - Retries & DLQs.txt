So now let's talk about retries and DLQs in EventBridge. So sometimes events cannot be delivered to a target due to several problems, for example, the target is not available or you have network issues. In that case, you can define a Retry Policy on EventBridge by configuring either a period of time, for example, 24 hours as a default and how many Retry attempts you want, the default is 185. So that means that 185 times the events will be tried to be redelivered to the targets. Then in case of all of this doesn't work so within the length of time and within the Retry attempts then the undelivered events will be sent to a Dead Letter Queue using SQS for later processing and you have access to that place to re process your events if you need to. And that's it, that's what you need to know for EventBridge for Retries and Dead Letter Queues. I hope you liked it and I will see you in the next lecture.
