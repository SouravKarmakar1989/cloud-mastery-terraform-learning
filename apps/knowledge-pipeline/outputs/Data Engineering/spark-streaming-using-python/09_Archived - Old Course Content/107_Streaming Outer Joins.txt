Welcome Back. In the earlier lectures, we learned to join a stream with a static dataframe. Then we also learned to join two streams. However, both the joins were inner joins. Spark Structured streaming also offers you to perform left and right outer joins, but full outer joins are not yet supported. However, you can perform left and right outer joins with some limitations. Let me explain. Stream to static Dataframe join is allowed with the following conditions. Left outer join is allowed if the left side is a stream. Right outer joins are allowed if the right side is a stream. Simple! Isn't it? If you want to perform an outer join, keep the stream on the left side, and apply the left outer. Similarly, keep the stream on the right side, and you can use the right outer join. Now let's come to the stream-to-stream joins. You can apply left-outer joins if you meet the following two conditions. You are implementing a watermark on the right-side stream. And you are implementing maximum time range constrain between the left and the right side events. Similarly, you can apply the right-outer join if you meet the following two conditions. You are implementing a watermark on the left-side stream. And you are enforcing maximum time range constraint between the left and the right side events. So, to simplify it, you have two rules to follow. Implement the watermark. Ideally, you should be implementing a watermark for left and right both. Correct? Otherwise, you won't be cleaning all states. So, I simply remember it as implementing a watermark is mandatory. That's all. The second rule is the same for the left and right both. So you must implement a time range constraint. Let's try to understand it with an example. We will again use the impression and click event example. Maximum time range between the left and the right side events What does it mean? They are asking us to define the maximum delay between the two events. Here is an example. Spark allows you to implement left or right outer joins on two streams. However, you must implement a watermark and time range condition. These two are mandatory requirements for Spark to implement an outer join. The watermark calculation for outer join also considers the time range condition. I showed you that. Right? One last thing. Unmatched records will arrive late because they will be waiting for the watermark boundary to expire. The unmatched record is further delayed by one micro-batch, and only appears in the next micro-batch because Spark computes the watermark after sending it.
