Welcome to a new section of the course. In this section, we are going to focus on Aggregate Functions in Spark. Spark provides a rich set of aggregate functions. First, we'll look at simple aggregate functions such as sum, average, min, max, etc.. We'll then look at doing aggregations by grouping data. We'll then learn about performing functions such as rank, lead, lag, etc., on a window of data which are called Window Functions. Finally, we'll apply all of these functions to our Formula 1 project to identify driver standings and constructors standings in a season. By the end of this section, I hope you will have a good understanding about various aggregate functions in Spark and also have the confidence to apply them in your project. So let's get started.
