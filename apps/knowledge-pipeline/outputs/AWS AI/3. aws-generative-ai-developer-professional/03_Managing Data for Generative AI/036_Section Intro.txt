Welcome to the section on Managing Data for generative AI. A critical part of building high quality, production ready gen AI systems. In this part of the course, we'll focus on how to prepare, manipulate, store, and optimize data that powers your models, vector stores, and knowledge bases. We'll begin with the fundamentals of working with structured data and walk through how to transform and prepare it using tools like Amazon Bedrock's data manipulation features, SageMaker, Data Wrangler, and AWS glue. You'll get hands on experience with Glue Studio glue, data quality, and using Amazon CloudWatch metrics to monitor your data pipelines. Then we'll explore how AWS services like Transcribe and Comprehend help process text and audio data, including an in-depth hands on look at entity extraction, sentiment analysis, and other pre-processing tasks that improve downstream model performance. From there, we'll dive into the core of AI data architecture vector databases. You'll learn how Amazon OpenSearch handles indexing performance, tuning stability, serverless deployments, and multimodal FME integrations. We'll also explore relational and NoSQL options, including Amazon RDS, Aurora with PKG, vector, and DynamoDB, and how they fit into AI workloads. Next, we'll cover how to keep your vector stores accurate and fresh, how to design advanced search architectures, and how to craft metadata frameworks that dramatically improve retrieval quality for Rag systems. Finally, we'll look at how to store and manage data efficiently with Amazon S3, including storage classes, lifecycle rules, and cross region replication, ensuring your data is secure, cost optimized, and highly available. By the end of this section, you'll have a complete understanding of how to manage the full data pipeline for generative AI, from ingestion and transformation to indexing, retrieval, and long term storage. Let's get started.
