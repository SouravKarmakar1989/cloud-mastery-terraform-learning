Welcome to the next part of the course, our series on other services you should know for the AWS Certified Generative AI Developer Professional exam. These upcoming sections focus on a wide range of AWS services that aren't central to generative AI itself, but are essential for designing complete, production ready AI solutions within real AWS environments. Now, while the exam guide does not group these services into specific content domains, they are explicitly listed as in scope for the exam, and they appear throughout AWS Skill Builder's recommended preparation materials. That means the exam expects you to understand what these services do, when to use them, and how they fit into the broader architecture surrounding your gen AI components. Think of this as the ecosystem around your foundation models analytics tools that process the data that you feed them. Compute and container services that run supporting applications, databases and caches that hold the information you retrieve. Machine learning services that complement bedrock and SageMaker. Developer tools that help you automate deployments, networking and delivery services that connect everything together. And storage systems that keep your data durable and accessible. These services aren't the star of the show, but they're vital supporting actors. Understanding them will help you design solutions that are well architected, cost efficient, secure, and tightly integrated with the rest of your AWS environment. This series will be presented mostly by Stefan, who will walk you through each of these categories and explain what you need to know for both the exam and real world AI system design. With that, let's hand things over to Stefan and explore the broader AWS ecosystem that surrounds your generative AI applications.
