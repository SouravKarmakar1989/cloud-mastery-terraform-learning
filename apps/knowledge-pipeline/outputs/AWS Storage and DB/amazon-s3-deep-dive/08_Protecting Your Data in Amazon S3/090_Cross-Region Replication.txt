So really quickly about cross region replication, why would you store your data in multiple bucket regions? Well, maybe compliance. So although Amazon S3 does store your data in multiple Azs within a region, well, maybe you have compliance requirements that dictate that you store data in even greater distances. I have worked with clients before that have very specific terms in regards to how far data centers and data needs to live. Some have been like 50 miles, some have been 500 or 1000 miles apart. Disasters wouldn't impact each one of those. So in order to satisfy those requirements, you can use cross region replication to replicate data between distant AWS regions. Now the next one is performance. So if you have customers into geographic locations or your applications live in multiple locations, well you can minimize the latency in accessing those objects by maintaining object copies in those different AWS regions that are geographically closer to your users. And finally, resiliency. Maybe you just want to maintain a secondary copy of your data in a different AWS region for operational resiliency. Just in the event that an AWS region becomes unavailable, you have your data living somewhere else. That's what cross region replication is and the benefits that you get from using cross region replication.
